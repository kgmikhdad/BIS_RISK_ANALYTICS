# Stress Testing Notes

Stress testing evaluates the resilience of a portfolio, institution, or system under severe but plausible adverse conditions. In a fixed-income portfolio, core scenario dimensions include the level of the yield curve, the slope of the yield curve, volatility, liquidity, credit spreads, and foreign exchange movements.

A useful stress scenario should specify the shock, the economic narrative, the transmission channel, the affected exposures, the risk metrics, and the validation checks. For market risk, common risk metrics include valuation loss, Value-at-Risk, Expected Shortfall, duration exposure, convexity exposure, drawdown, and breach frequency.

Interest-rate stress can be represented through a parallel yield-curve shift, a steepening scenario, a flattening scenario, or a sudden repricing event. Liquidity stress can be represented through bid-ask spread widening, higher price impact, reduced market depth, and forced selling.

A good stress-testing workflow separates baseline valuation, deterministic scenario analysis, stochastic simulation, sensitivity analysis, model validation, governance, and reporting.
